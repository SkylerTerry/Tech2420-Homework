Web Site Critiques

INFO 2420 — Project 1 • Prepared September 15, 2025

Question List

  1. What was your first impression of this site?
  2. How does this site organize the flow of information?
  3. Does the site structure make sense or does it confuse you? Why?
  4. Does the site graphics/layout/multimedia enhance the site or detract from it? Why?
  5. How long did it take for the home page to load? Was the load time reasonable?
  6. Was the text color and size readable? If a background color or image was used, did it enhance or detract from the site?
  7. Did the site have a navigation bar or other navigation tool? Was the navigation well done?
  8. Did the site have a color scheme or look and feel that was repeated on all the pages?
  9. How does the site respond to smaller window sizes (responsive design)?
  10. What does this site offer that was unusual or very well done?

Site Review

Trader Joe's — https://www.traderjoes.com

1) First impression:

The site felt simple, but put together well.

2) Information flow:

Most of the flow is from the drop down in the top left, and the front page is almost blog-style esque.

3) Structure makes sense?

I don't love it because you have to click on things. I would like pop-ups as I hover over them. Also, I don't love the intro page; I want to see something visual on the front page that directs me in the right direction.

4) Graphics/layout/multimedia:

The background and the background of the shapes on top are the same, making it feel clunky. Black on black or white on white isn't great to me — they should be different shades so the front image stands out.

5) Load time:

It loaded near instantly. Totally happy with it.

6) Readability & background:

Most sites are actually smaller or larger than I like to see, but Trader Joe's was pleasantly in the middle.

7) Navigation quality:

I never use search bars unless I can't find something because they're usually terrible. In this case I felt like it wasn't super useful, as I kept getting blog-post type results instead of products.

8) Consistent look & feel:

The scheme felt warm and inviting, like a grandma's kitchen. I suspect Trader Joe's wanted that on purpose.

9) Responsive behavior:

Surprisingly well done. I didn't have to use the horizontal scroll bar; it resized well until extremely small window sizes.

10) Notable strengths:

I see a lot of sites with popups, but these popups were all colored differently, which almost didn't fit the vibe of the site. I especially found it frustrating finding the X's to shut the popups off.
